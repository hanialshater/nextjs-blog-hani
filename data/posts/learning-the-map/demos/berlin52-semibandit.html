Thick faint edges on the map are roads this policy is still uncertain about; thin solid edges are well measured. Click a legend entry to show that policy on the map.

True road cost = Euclidean distance (TSPLIB berlin52); each drive returns that cost times multiplicative noise. The optimizer is nearest-neighbour + 2-opt run on each policy's view of the map: random ignores the map, greedy trusts its current means, UCB makes unmeasured roads look temporarily cheaper (the minus sign in mean − β·√(log t / n)), and Thompson solves one plausible sampled map. Watch greedy lock onto an early false shortcut while the semi-bandits keep probing uncertainty and slide toward the oracle line.
