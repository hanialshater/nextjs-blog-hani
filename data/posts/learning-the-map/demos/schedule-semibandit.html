Columns = experts (header colour = specialty), rows = Mon–Fri × AM/PM. Grey cells = expert unavailable. A filled dot = a booked client, coloured by client type; brighter = the policy rates that booking higher. Click a legend entry to show that policy's week.

Booking quality = a hidden client-type × expert-specialty compatibility plus a per-booking quirk, seen with noise only when the booking is made. Each week the optimizer solves the exact max-weight assignment of clients to open slots (the in-browser analogue of an LP in Pyomo/CVXPY) on each policy's current estimates. Availability removes most cells, so "just try every client in every slot" is expensive — which is exactly when structure pays: Contextual + per-pair learns the small type×specialty table (one good booking teaches it about every similar client) then a per-booking correction, and pulls ahead of the tabular policies that must probe almost every client·slot pair. Greedy locks onto an early guess; random never learns.
