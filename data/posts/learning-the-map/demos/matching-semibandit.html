Semi-bandit matching — assign customers to experts while learning who fits whom

Each round you must assign all customers to experts (one each). You only learn the quality of the pairings you actually make. Four policies race to the best total match.

Left = customers (colour = type), right = experts (colour = specialty). Lines show the current assignment; brighter = the policy rates that pairing higher. Click a legend entry to show that policy.

True pairing quality = a hidden type × specialty compatibility (some kinds of customer just fit some kinds of expert) plus a per-pair quirk unique to each customer–expert couple, observed with noise only when the pairing is actually made. The optimizer is the exact Hungarian assignment (the in-browser analogue of an LP solved with Pyomo/CVXPY) run on each policy's current value estimates: random ignores history, greedy trusts its current per-pair means, UCB adds an optimism bonus per pair, and Contextual + per-pair learns the small type×specialty table first (so one good match teaches it about every similar customer) then a per-couple correction. Contextual converges far sooner from far fewer pairings; the tabular policies must try almost every couple. Same pattern as the routing demo: a solver wrapped around uncertainty.
